References#

This page provides a comprehensive bibliography of the academic literature underlying PrefGraph, organized by topic.

Note

The core reference for PrefGraph is Chambers & Echenique (2016), Revealed Preference Theory. Chapter numbers throughout the documentation refer to this textbook.

Selected Reading#

Recent applied work using revealed preference methods on real-world data:

  • Choi et al. (2014) — Lab CCEI benchmarks (≈0.88). American Economic Review (experiment).

  • “Revealed preference theory: An algorithmic outlook” (2019). European Journal of Operational Research.

  • Deb, Kitamura, Quah & Stoye (2023) — Revealed price preference: Theory and empirical analysis. Review of Economic Studies, 90(2), 707–743.

  • “The Moral Mind(s) of Large Language Models” (arXiv, 2024/2025).

  • Devine et al. (2025) — Context effects in economic choice: A meta-analysis. Working paper.

  • GARP‑EFM (2026) — “GARP‑EFM: Improving Foundation Models with Revealed Preference Structure.” arXiv (March 2026).

Foundational Works#

Core Textbook:

Classical Foundations:

  • Afriat, S. N. (1967). The construction of utility functions from expenditure data. International Economic Review, 8(1), 67-77. [Paper]

  • Samuelson, P. A. (1938). A note on the pure theory of consumer’s behaviour. Economica, 5(17), 61-71. [Paper]

  • Richter, M. K. (1966). Revealed preference theory. Econometrica, 34(3), 635-645. [Paper]

  • Hicks, J. R. (1939). Value and Capital. Oxford University Press.

Efficiency Measures:

Test Power:

Menu-Based Choice Theory#

Abstract Choice:

  • Arrow, K. J. (1959). Rational choice functions and orderings. Economica, 26(102), 121-127.

  • Sen, A. K. (1971). Choice functions and revealed preference. Review of Economic Studies, 38(3), 307-317.

Experimental Evidence:

Limited Attention and Consideration Sets#

Deterministic Attention:

  • Masatlioglu, Y., Nakajima, D., & Ozbay, E. Y. (2012). Revealed Attention. American Economic Review, 102(5), 2183-2205. [DOI]

    Key contribution: Introduces WARP(LA), characterizing choice with limited attention via attention filters. Shows that WARP violations can be rationalized by assuming consumers don’t see all options.

  • Lleras, J. S., Masatlioglu, Y., Nakajima, D., & Ozbay, E. Y. (2017). When more is less: Limited consideration. Journal of Economic Theory, 170, 70-85. [DOI]

Stochastic Consideration:

  • Manzini, P., & Mariotti, M. (2014). Stochastic choice and consideration sets. Econometrica, 82(3), 1153-1176. [DOI]

Random Attention Model:

  • Cattaneo, M. D., Ma, X., Masatlioglu, Y., & Suleymanov, E. (2020). A Random Attention Model. Journal of Political Economy, 128(7), 2796-2836. [DOI]

    Key contribution: Extends attention models to stochastic choice. Provides testable restrictions and bounds on attention probabilities from choice frequency data.

Stochastic Choice and Random Utility#

Foundational Theory:

  • McFadden, D. (1974). Conditional logit analysis of qualitative choice behavior. In P. Zarembka (Ed.), Frontiers in Econometrics (pp. 105-142). Academic Press.

  • Luce, R. D. (1959). Individual Choice Behavior: A Theoretical Analysis. Wiley.

Modern Developments:

  • Smeulders, B., Crama, Y., & Spieksma, F. C. R. (2021). Revealed preference tests of collectively rational consumption behavior: Formulations and algorithms. Operations Research, 69(4), 1237-1253. [DOI]

IIA and Regularity:

  • Debreu, G. (1960). Review of R. D. Luce, Individual choice behavior. American Economic Review, 50, 186-188.

  • Fudenberg, D., Iijima, R., & Strzalecki, T. (2015). Stochastic choice and revealed perturbed utility. Econometrica, 83(6), 2371-2409. [DOI]
