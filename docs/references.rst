References
==========

This page provides a comprehensive bibliography of the academic literature underlying PyRevealed,
organized by topic.

.. note::

   The core reference for PyRevealed is Chambers & Echenique (2016), *Revealed Preference Theory*.
   Chapter numbers throughout the documentation refer to this textbook.

------------------

Limited Attention and Consideration Sets
----------------------------------------

**Deterministic Attention:**

- Masatlioglu, Y., Nakajima, D., & Ozbay, E. Y. (2012). Revealed Attention.
  *American Economic Review*, 102(5), 2183-2205.
  `[DOI] <https://doi.org/10.1257/aer.102.5.2183>`__

  *Key contribution: Introduces WARP(LA), characterizing choice with limited attention via
  attention filters. Shows that WARP violations can be rationalized by assuming consumers
  don't see all options.*

- Lleras, J. S., Masatlioglu, Y., Nakajima, D., & Ozbay, E. Y. (2017).
  When more is less: Limited consideration.
  *Journal of Economic Theory*, 170, 70-85.
  `[DOI] <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jet.2017.04.004>`__

**Stochastic Consideration:**

- Manzini, P., & Mariotti, M. (2014). Stochastic choice and consideration sets.
  *Econometrica*, 82(3), 1153-1176.
  `[DOI] <https://doi.org/10.3982/ECTA10575>`__

**Random Attention Model:**

- Cattaneo, M. D., Ma, X., Masatlioglu, Y., & Suleymanov, E. (2020).
  A Random Attention Model.
  *Journal of Political Economy*, 128(7), 2796-2836.
  `[DOI] <https://doi.org/10.1086/706861>`__

  *Key contribution: Extends attention models to stochastic choice. Provides testable
  restrictions and bounds on attention probabilities from choice frequency data.*
