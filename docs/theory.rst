Overview
--------

**Budget-Based Analysis (Chapters 3-5):**

- :doc:`theory_foundations` - Notation, maintained assumptions, and axiom hierarchy
- :doc:`theory_consistency` - GARP, WARP, SARP and related consistency tests
- :doc:`theory_efficiency` - AEI, MPI, Houtman-Maks, and test power metrics

**Preference Structure (Chapter 4):**

- :doc:`theory_structure` - HARP, separability, utility recovery

**Menu-Based Analysis (Chapters 1-2):**

- :doc:`theory_abstract` - Abstract choice theory without prices

**Advanced Topics (Chapters 6-15):**

- :doc:`theory_advanced` - Integrability, welfare analysis, compensated demand
- :doc:`theory_extensions` - Spatial preferences, stochastic choice, limited attention, production

References
----------

See the full :doc:`references` page for the complete bibliography with links.

**Key works referenced throughout this documentation:**

- Chambers, C. P., & Echenique, F. (2016). *Revealed Preference Theory*. Cambridge University Press.
  The core textbook underlying PyRevealed.

- Afriat, S. N. (1967). The construction of utility functions from expenditure data.
  *International Economic Review*. Foundation for GARP and utility recovery.

- Varian, H. R. (1982, 1983). The nonparametric approach to demand analysis.
  *Econometrica* and *Review of Economic Studies*. Modern foundations of GARP testing.

- Masatlioglu, Y., Nakajima, D., & Ozbay, E. Y. (2012). Revealed attention.
  *American Economic Review*. WARP(LA) and attention filter theory.

- Cattaneo, M. D., Ma, X., Masatlioglu, Y., & Suleymanov, E. (2020). A Random Attention Model.
  *Journal of Political Economy*. RAM for stochastic choice with limited attention.

- Block, H. D., & Marschak, J. (1960). Random orderings and stochastic theories of responses.
  *Contributions to Probability and Statistics*. Foundation for RUM testing.

- McFadden, D. (1974). Conditional logit analysis of qualitative choice behavior.
  *Frontiers in Econometrics*. Logit model and IIA.

- Houtman, M., & Maks, J. A. H. (1985). Determining all maximal data subsets consistent
  with revealed preference. *Kwantitatieve Methoden*. Houtman-Maks efficiency index.

- Echenique, F., Lee, S., & Shum, M. (2011). The Money Pump as a measure of revealed
  preference violations. *Journal of Political Economy*. Money Pump Index.
